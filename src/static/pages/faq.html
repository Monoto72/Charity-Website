Frequently Asked Questions

A charity shop is a building run by an organisation where you can donate unwanted items in person. A clothes bin (often referred to as a textile bin or a donation bin) is a public container in which items of clothes can be placed inside to be regularly collected by the charity organisation which it is associcated with.

Charity shops are ideal for items in good condition to be sold in the store. It is also able to accept non-clothing items such as furniture, books and toys.

Clothes bins are ideal for large quantities of clothes, including items that are in imperfect condition to be recycled for textiles. However be sure to follow the guidelines on what items and what condition they can be donated in.

Drip Drop uses The Salvation Army's list of donation spots and Google Maps for other organisations. As well as this, we take user suggested locations for unlisted spots.
